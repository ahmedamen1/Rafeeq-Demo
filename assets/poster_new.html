Abstract
Smart Wheelchair Kit (Rafeeq)
Motivation
Over one million people in Egypt use wheelchairs and face difficulties moving independently. Imported smart wheelchairs are too expensive and do not support Arabic.
Problem
Most smart wheelchairs cost $5,000–$10,000 — too high for most Egyptians. They rely on cloud services and offer no Arabic support.
Proposed Solution
Rafeeq (SmartWheel KIT) — a low-cost modular kit that converts a manual wheelchair into a smart autonomous one. Target: 40,000–50,000 EGP, ~70% cheaper than imports.
Methods
Built on Raspberry Pi 5 with LiDAR + Kinect 360 for autonomous navigation. Offline Arabic voice recognition. Health sensors (HR, SpO₂, temp) streamed to a Flutter caregiver app with Arabic RAG chatbot.
Results
All six motion directions verified. 87% offline Arabic voice accuracy, 0.9 s latency. Health sensors upload confirmed. Patient and caregiver app interfaces fully operational.
Conclusion
Rafeeq demonstrates that advanced wheelchair technology can be affordable and adapted to local needs using modular design, offline AI, and Arabic language support.

01 · Why Rafeeq?
Motivation & Context

Over 80 million people globally require wheelchair assistance. Egypt has approximately 11–12 million citizens with disabilities and an estimated 1 million active wheelchair users, yet 99.35% lack access to smart assistive technology.

The Critical Gap
  • Economic Exclusion: Global smart wheelchairs cost $5,000–$10,000 (157,000–315,000 EGP) — 20–50 months of median Egyptian household income
  • Cultural Disconnect: Zero commercially available systems support Arabic; 89% of Egyptian elderly function primarily in Arabic
  • Limited Intelligence: Existing solutions focus only on powered mobility, ignoring health monitoring and emotional support needs
Vision

A modular AI-powered retrofit kit that transforms existing manual wheelchairs into intelligent mobility companions at 60–70% lower cost than imports (25,000–45,000 EGP vs. 100,000–300,000 EGP).

04 · Implementation
Technical Architecture & Design Decisions
Voice AI — Offline Arabic ASR

Offline Arabic speech recognition using faster-whisper INT8 (Whisper-small) fine-tuned on user recordings. Pipeline: USB mic (16 kHz) → noise suppression → model inference → motion command dispatch. 87% command recognition accuracy · 0.9 s average latency · fully offline — no internet required. Cloud alternatives (Google ASR, AWS) were rejected: violate Egyptian Law 151/2020 by transmitting audio to foreign servers.

Autonomous Navigation — ROS 2 + Nav2

RPLIDAR A1M8 (2D, 12 m) feeds SLAM Toolbox to build and save occupancy grid maps. Microsoft Kinect 360 adds a 3D voxel layer for above-floor obstacle detection. Nav2 Behavior Tree manages path planning, localization, and recovery. A scan_relay ROS 2 node filters raw LiDAR noise before publishing to /scan. Operates reliably in complete darkness (LiDAR-only).

Mechanical — 5-Iteration Motor Mount

Converting a manual wheelchair to a powered platform required 5 design iterations: single bent-plate bracket (misaligned) → box-section → independent suspension redesign → final validated mount. Supports 120 kg user load with even drive-wheel contact on indoor terrain.

Mobile Application — Flutter + Firebase

Cross-platform app (Android/iOS) with separate Patient and Caregiver interfaces.
