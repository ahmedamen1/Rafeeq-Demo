Abstract
Smart Wheelchair Kit (Rafeeq) — AI-Powered Modular Retrofit System
Motivation
Over one million people in Egypt use wheelchairs and face difficulties moving independently. Imported smart wheelchairs cost $5,000–$10,000 and do not support Arabic — inaccessible for most Egyptian families.
Problem
No affordable, Arabic-first, offline smart wheelchair exists. Existing solutions are cloud-dependent, English-only, and priced at 157,000–315,000 EGP — far beyond reach of middle-income Egyptian households.
Proposed Solution
Rafeeq (SmartWheel KIT) — a modular retrofit kit that converts any existing manual wheelchair into a smart autonomous platform. Target price: 40,000–50,000 EGP, approximately 70% cheaper than imported alternatives.
Methods
Built on a Raspberry Pi 5 (8 GB) running ROS 2 Jazzy. RPLIDAR A1M8 + Kinect 360 for SLAM navigation. Offline Arabic CNN (150 KB TFLite) for voice. MAX30100 + DS18B20 health sensors. Flutter caregiver app with Firebase. GPT-4o-mini RAG chatbot with Arabic TTS/STT.
Results
All six motion directions verified via joystick and voice. CNN achieved 77.1% overall accuracy, 98% on move_forward, <10 ms latency. Health sensors and Firebase upload confirmed. Patient and caregiver app interfaces fully operational. Chatbot pipeline verified, ~6 s round-trip.
Conclusion
Rafeeq demonstrates that advanced wheelchair technology can be affordable and culturally adapted. Modular design, offline AI, and Arabic-first interaction deliver a practical, dignified, and privacy-compliant mobility solution for Egypt's 1M+ wheelchair users at 10% of import cost.

01 · Motivation
Why Rafeeq?
Critical Gap: 99.35% of Egypt's 1 million wheelchair users lack access to smart wheelchair technology. The global smart wheelchair market is valued at \$4.8 billion (2022), yet zero products support Arabic language interfaces.
  • Economic Exclusion: Imported smart wheelchairs cost 157,000–315,000 EGP — unreachable for middle-income Egyptian families earning 6,000–8,000 EGP/month.
  • Cultural Disconnect: All existing systems use English, Mandarin, or Japanese interfaces. 89% of Egyptian elderly users function exclusively in Arabic.
  • Limited Intelligence: Commercial solutions focus only on powered mobility, ignoring health monitoring, emotional support, and caregiver coordination.
  • Caregiver Burden: 58% of family caregivers of wheelchair-dependent adults report high emotional stress; many reduce employment to provide care (Darragh et al., 2015).
  • User Wellbeing: 57.1% prevalence of low life satisfaction among wheelchair users in Egypt, with environmental barriers as a primary predictor (Nour-Eldein et al., 2024).
Vision: Rafeeq democratizes intelligent mobility through modular design, offline AI, and Arabic-first interaction — achieving 60–70% cost reduction over imported alternatives.

05 · Implementation
Technical Architecture & Design Decisions

Voice AI — CNN Audio Classifier

TFLite CNN on 10 Egyptian Arabic commands. Pipeline: USB mic → noise suppression → MFCC+Δ+ΔΔ (13×47×3) → inference. 77.1% accuracy · <10 ms · 150 KB · fully offline.

Autonomous Navigation — ROS 2 + Nav2

RPLIDAR A1M8 feeds SLAM Toolbox for occupancy maps. Kinect 360 adds 3D voxel layer for above-floor obstacles. Nav2 Behavior Tree handles planning, localization & recovery via ROS 2 Topics and TF2.

Mechanical — 5-Iteration Motor Mount

5 design iterations to validated mount supporting 120 kg load with even drive-wheel contact on indoor terrain carrying two 350 W BLDC hub motors.

Mobile Application — Flutter + Firebase

Patient & Caregiver interfaces. Flutter + Firebase Realtime Database + Auth.

Live Health DashboardReal-time HR, SpO₂ & temperature from ESP32 sensors

AI Chatbot — RAG Architecture

Arabic voice pipeline: Whisper STT → ChromaDB RAG (76 chunks) → GPT-4o-mini → ElevenLabs TTS. End-to-end latency ~6 s.
