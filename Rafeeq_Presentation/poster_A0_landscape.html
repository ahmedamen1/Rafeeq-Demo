Abstract
Smart Wheelchair Kit (Rafeeq) — AI-Powered Modular Retrofit System
Motivation
Over 1M people in Egypt use wheelchairs. Imported smart chairs cost $5,000–$10,000 and lack Arabic support — inaccessible for most Egyptians.
Problem
No affordable, Arabic-first, offline smart wheelchair exists. Existing solutions are cloud-dependent, English-only, and priced beyond reach of Egyptian middle-income families.
Proposed Solution
Rafeeq — a modular retrofit kit converting any manual wheelchair into a smart, autonomous platform at 40,000–50,000 EGP, 70% cheaper than imports.
Methods
Built on a Raspberry Pi 5 (8 GB) running ROS 2 Jazzy. Sensors: RPLIDAR A1M8 + Kinect 360 for SLAM navigation. Offline Arabic CNN (TFLite, 150 KB) for voice. MAX30100 + DS18B20 for health. Flutter app for caregivers. GPT-4o-mini + ChromaDB RAG chatbot with Arabic TTS/STT.
Results
All 6 motion directions verified via joystick and voice. CNN classifier achieved 77.1% overall accuracy and 98% on move_forward. Health sensors and Firebase upload confirmed operational. Both patient and caregiver app interfaces fully functional. Chatbot pipeline verified end-to-end with ~6 s round-trip latency.
Conclusion
Rafeeq demonstrates that advanced wheelchair technology can be affordable and culturally adapted. By combining modular hardware, offline AI, Egyptian Arabic voice control, real-time health monitoring, and caregiver coordination, it delivers a practical, dignified, and privacy-compliant mobility solution at 10% of import cost.

01 · Motivation
Why Rafeeq?
Critical Gap: 99.35% of Egypt's 1M wheelchair users lack access to smart wheelchair technology. The global smart wheelchair market is valued at $4.8B (2022), yet zero products support Arabic interfaces.
  • Economic Exclusion: Imported smart wheelchairs cost 157,000–315,000 EGP — unreachable for families earning 6,000–8,000 EGP/month.
  • Cultural Disconnect: All existing systems use English, Mandarin, or Japanese. 89% of Egyptian elderly users function exclusively in Arabic.
  • Limited Intelligence: Commercial solutions focus only on powered mobility, ignoring health monitoring, emotional support, and caregiver coordination.
  • Caregiver Burden: 58% of family caregivers of wheelchair-dependent adults report high emotional stress (Darragh et al., 2015).
  • User Wellbeing: 57.1% prevalence of low life satisfaction among wheelchair users in Egypt (Nour-Eldein et al., 2024).
Vision: Democratize intelligent mobility via modular design, offline AI, and Arabic-first interaction at 60–70% cost reduction.

05 · Implementation
Technical Architecture & Design Decisions
Voice AI — CNN Audio Classifier

Custom TFLite CNN trained on 10 Egyptian Arabic commands. Pipeline: USB mic (16 kHz) → 4-stage noise suppression → MFCC+Δ+ΔΔ tensor (13×47×3) → CNN. 77.1% accuracy · <10 ms latency · 150 KB model · fully offline. Rejected: Vosk (~300 MB, 5 s) and Whisper.cpp (~430 MB, 120 s).

Autonomous Navigation — ROS 2 + Nav2

RPLIDAR A1M8 feeds SLAM Toolbox for occupancy grid maps. Kinect 360 provides 3D voxel obstacle layer. Nav2 Behavior Tree manages path planning and recovery. scan_relay node filters LiDAR noise before publishing to /scan.

Mechanical — 5-Iteration Motor Mount

5 design iterations from single bent-plate → box-section → independent suspension → final validated mount. Supports 120 kg user load with two 350 W BLDC hub motors.
