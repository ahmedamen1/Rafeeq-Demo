Go-to-Market Strategy

Credibility first. Scale second. Consumer third.

A deliberately sequenced three-phase rollout — institutional validation before moving to distribution networks, distribution networks before direct-to-consumer. Each phase de-risks the next.

01
B2B
Hospitals & Clinics
Fast multi-unit deals with immediate institutional credibility. Entry point for clinical data collection and ISF reporting.
  • Target public rehabilitation centres in Cairo & Giza
  • 3–5 pilot units per facility as proof-of-deployment
  • Collect clinical usage data & patient testimonials
  • Ministry of Health anchor partnership pathway
02
B2B2C
Medical Distributors
Leverage existing healthcare distribution networks to scale reach without building a direct sales force — using established trust channels.
  • Partner with disability-focused NGOs (e.g. Masr El Khair)
  • Medical supply stores & pharmacy chain agreements
  • Per-unit revenue share model with distributors
  • Subsidised kits via NGO and charity funding pools
03
B2C
Families Direct
Consumer-direct channel activated after social proof and institutional validation. HaaS model makes the Gamma tier accessible to middle-income families.
  • Social media campaigns with real user video testimonials
  • HaaS model: 3,000 EGP/month — no upfront cost
  • Target households earning 8,000–25,000 EGP/month
  • Online ordering with local installation support

Glossary of Terms

Abbreviations explained.

Every abbreviation used in this document is defined here in plain language — no background knowledge assumed.

TAM
Total Addressable Market
The full revenue potential if every possible customer adopted the product. For RAFEEQ: ~1M Egyptian wheelchair users × 25,000 EGP = 25B EGP.
SAM
Serviceable Addressable Market
The portion of the TAM that RAFEEQ can realistically reach given its price point, product fit, and distribution capability. ~70,000 middle-income households = 1.75B EGP.
SOM
Serviceable Obtainable Market
The realistic short-term revenue target — what RAFEEQ can actually capture in its first year through its pilot B2B channel. 100 units = 2.5M EGP.
CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate
How fast a market grows per year, expressed as a percentage. The MEA smart wheelchair market grows at 19.33% CAGR (2025–2033), meaning it nearly doubles every 4 years.
B2B
Business to Business
Selling directly to organisations rather than individuals. For RAFEEQ: hospitals, rehabilitation centres, and clinics purchasing multiple units for patient use.
B2B2C
Business to Business to Consumer
Distributing through a business intermediary that then sells to end consumers. For RAFEEQ: medical distributors and NGOs selling kits to families on RAFEEQ's behalf.
B2C
Business to Consumer
Selling directly to individual families without an intermediary. RAFEEQ's Phase 3 channel — online orders with local installation, made accessible via the HaaS model.
HaaS
Hardware as a Service
Renting physical hardware on a monthly subscription instead of selling it outright. RAFEEQ offers this at 3,000 EGP/month, removing the 70,000+ EGP upfront cost barrier.
SaaS
Software as a Service
Software delivered via the internet on a recurring subscription. RAFEEQ's caregiver app, AI assistant, and health dashboard are the SaaS layer — 1,500 EGP/year per user.
MRR
Monthly Recurring Revenue
Predictable monthly income from active subscriptions or rental contracts. Grows automatically as more units are deployed in the field — without additional sales effort.
LiDAR
Light Detection and Ranging
A laser-based sensor that measures distance to surrounding objects by timing reflected light pulses. RAFEEQ uses an RPLidar A1M8 to detect walls, furniture, and people.
SLAM
Simultaneous Localisation and Mapping
The technique that lets a robot build a map of its environment while simultaneously tracking its own position within that map. RAFEEQ uses SLAM Toolbox on ROS2 for indoor navigation.
ASR
Automatic Speech Recognition
Software that converts spoken audio into text commands. RAFEEQ uses the Vosk ASR engine running fully offline on the Raspberry Pi 5 — no internet connection required.
RAG
Retrieval-Augmented Generation
An AI technique that answers questions by first retrieving relevant information from a knowledge base, then generating a response. RAFEEQ's Arabic AI companion uses this to give contextually accurate answers.
ROS2
Robot Operating System 2
The open-source middleware framework used to coordinate RAFEEQ's sensors, navigation, and control systems. RAFEEQ runs the Jazzy Jalisco release on Ubuntu 24.04.
PCB
Printed Circuit Board
A single manufactured board that consolidates multiple electronic components, wiring, and connectors. A custom PCB is planned in Phase 2 to reduce BOM cost and improve reliability.
BOM
Bill of Materials
The complete itemised list of every component needed to build one unit, with costs and quantities. RAFEEQ's BOM excludes the wheelchair chassis itself — only the retrofit kit components are counted.
ISF
Innovators Support Fund
Egyptian government grant programme — "صندوق رعاية المبتكرين والنوابغ" — funding deep-tech student projects with commercialisation potential. RAFEEQ received 80,000 EGP.
MEA
Middle East and Africa
The geographic region that forms RAFEEQ's medium-term expansion market beyond Egypt. The MEA smart wheelchair market grows at 19.33% CAGR (2025–2033).
NGO
Non-Governmental Organisation
A non-profit organisation independent of government. RAFEEQ's B2B2C strategy includes partnerships with disability-focused NGOs such as Masr El Khair to subsidise kit access.
SDG
Sustainable Development Goal
The 17 goals set by the United Nations to create a better, more sustainable world by 2030. RAFEEQ aligns with SDG 3 (Good Health), SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities), and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities).
SOS
Emergency Distress Signal
Universally recognised emergency signal. In RAFEEQ, pressing the SOS button or an automatic trigger sends an immediate alert to the caregiver's phone via the mobile app.
FOC
Field-Oriented Control
An advanced motor control technique that independently controls a motor's torque and speed for smooth, efficient movement. RAFEEQ's STM32 microcontroller runs FOC firmware for the two hub motors.
BLDC
Brushless DC Motor
An efficient, low-maintenance electric motor. RAFEEQ uses two 350W BLDC 8-inch hub motors (hoverboard-style) for reliable, smooth wheelchair propulsion.
