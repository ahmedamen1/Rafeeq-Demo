The Problem We're Solving

Egypt has over 1 million wheelchair users — yet 99.35% have no access to advanced assistive technology. Rafeeq changes that.

Problem
No Independence
Manual wheelchair users depend entirely on caregivers for every movement. Standard chairs offer zero autonomy.
Problem
No Health Monitoring
Caregivers can't track patient vitals remotely. Critical health events go undetected until it's too late.
Problem
Global Solutions Don't Fit
Existing assistive devices are built for Western markets — too expensive, not in Arabic, and not designed for Egyptian homes.
Solution
Rafeeq Adds Intelligence
6 integrated modules that retrofit onto any existing wheelchair — no replacement needed. Egyptian Arabic first, built for local homes.

01
Subsystem

Mechanical Design

Supporting a 120 kg user on uneven indoor floors while attaching modularly to a frame never designed for motor torque. We didn't solve this in one pass — we iterated five times.

MotorBLDC Hub, 350W × 2
Torque11 N·m per motor
Voltage36V Li-ion
ControlFOC + Hall sensors
Wheel8 inch drive wheels
Camber0° — zero deflection
"We didn't design the final mount in a single pass. We iterated five times."
Iter 1Bent-plate bracket — rejected
Iter 2Stainless steel box-section — rejected
Iter 3Mild-steel flat-bar bracket — partial
Iter 4Independent suspension CAD — validated
Iter 5Manufactured mount — alignment corrected
CNC milling ±0.05mm · Wire-cut EDM for motor mount profiling — designed and machined in Egypt

Subsystem

Autonomous Navigation

Maps your home once. Navigates forever. No GPS — fully indoor, offline, obstacle-aware. LiDAR builds a 5 cm/cell occupancy grid; MPPI evaluates 10,000 trajectories per cycle to dodge obstacles in real time.
